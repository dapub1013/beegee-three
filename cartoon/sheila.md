# Notes

**Mage Hand Legerdemain:** Arcane Trickster's version of Mage Hand can pick locks, disarm traps, and steal items from a distance without Sheila being in range. This is the build's defining utility feature — use it before every chest and trap encounter rather than walking Sheila into danger.

**Invisibility cloak fantasy:** Sheila's cloak is her signature. Shade-Slayer Cloak (Act 3) makes her invisible when she enters shadows. Cloak of Cunning Brume (Act 2) creates a smoke cloud on Disengage. Shadow-Cloaked Ring grants Sneak Attack while in dim light or darkness. The build leans into all of these — position her in shadow, attack from concealment, vanish after.

**Sneak Attack:** At level 12 Sneak Attack deals 6d6 bonus damage on qualifying hits. Sheila needs advantage or an adjacent ally to proc it. The Shadow-Cloaked Ring removes the adjacency requirement in dim light. Greater Invisibility (level 10 spell) grants advantage on all attacks while invisible — combine with a high-damage strike to delete a priority target.

**Alert feat:** Like Presto, Sheila dies if she acts after enemies spot her. Alert ensures she gets Stealth and positioning before the fight starts properly. Invisibility on round one is only useful if she gets to cast it before anyone locks onto her.

**Gloves of Dexterity transition:** Grab these at Crèche Y'llek and immediately reallocate — dump the points that were in DEX into CON and INT. Sheila's spell save DC and Arcane Trickster spell attacks scale with INT, so the reallocation genuinely improves her control spells.

**Hold Person into Sneak Attack:** Held targets are paralyzed, and melee attacks against paralyzed creatures automatically crit. Sheila cannot deliver the melee attack herself reliably, but she can hold a target and let Diana or Bobby walk up for a guaranteed critical. Coordinate this in fights against named humanoid enemies.
