# Notes

**Colossus Slayer:** Deals an extra 1d8 damage once per turn when the target is below its maximum hit points. Because enemies rarely enter combat at full health (previous hits, environmental damage, or another party member going first), this triggers reliably every turn from the second round onward. It stacks with Hunter's Mark's 1d6 on every hit and Sharpshooter's +10, making sustained single-target damage strong throughout Acts 1 and 2 before Volley arrives.

**Volley:** Hank's level 11 capstone. Makes one ranged attack roll against every creature within 9 feet of a target point. With Titanstring Bow and a Hill Giant Strength elixir, every arrow adds +5 from Strength plus any other bonuses. Hunter's Mark adds 1d6 to each hit. Sharpshooter's –5/+10 can be toggled per use of Volley: take the penalty against clustered trash, drop it against elites with high AC. This is the build's defining move in Act 3 and the payoff for staying pure Ranger to 12.

**Lightning Arrow:** Available at Ranger level 9 — only possible in this pure build, not the v1 multiclass. Converts one arrow into a bolt of lightning dealing 3d8 to the target and 2d8 to nearby creatures on a miss. Use it to open a Volley round for free splash damage, or against grouped enemies where the secondary hits add up. The energy bow lore maps cleanly onto this spell.

**Sharpshooter math:** The –5/+10 tradeoff is almost always worth taking at level 12 (Dex 20, Archery +2 = +12 to hit before magic weapon bonus). Against typical Tactician AC, missing only on a 1–3 while adding +10 damage per hit is a straightforward net gain. Hold off on the penalty against enemies with unusually high AC (Cazador, Raphael) until they are debuffed or flanked.

**Pass without Trace:** +10 to Stealth for the whole party. This effectively replaces the Gloom Stalker's natural stealth advantage and lets the party set up ambushes from the surprise round regardless of who is in the group. Use before any major room clear in Act 2 and 3.

**Mage Breaker / Sanctified Stalker:** Mage Breaker gives advantage on attack rolls against any enemy concentrating on a spell — which in BG3 is a large proportion of mid and late-game casters. With multiple attacks per round, Hank has several chances to break concentration before it becomes a problem. Sanctified Stalker helps protect Hunter's Mark and Spike Growth concentration from the other side.

**Titanstring Bow + elixirs:** The Strength modifier from elixirs applies directly to Titanstring's damage. This is most impactful at level 11 with Volley: a five-target Volley with +5 Strength bonus adds +25 damage across the group before Sharpshooter, Hunter's Mark, or Colossus Slayer are counted. Stock elixirs aggressively; they are cheap and widely available from merchants throughout the game.

**Escape the Horde:** Opportunity attacks against Hank are made with disadvantage. This lets him reposition freely between turns — critical for placing Volley at the best angle — without eating punishing free attacks from melee enemies. Pair with Boots of Speed's Momentum to cover ground quickly in the same action economy.
