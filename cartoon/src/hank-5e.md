# Hank, the Ranger (5e)

The oldest among the kids, Hank acts as their leader throughout their adventures in the Realm. Self-disciplined and dependable, Hank leads because the others respect his judgment, not because he's bossy or imperious. Hank's greatest fear is that he'll do something to lose his friends' respect and thus lose their friendship.

## Class Features

### Favored Enemy
Hank has studied and hunted two types of enemies:
- **Humanoids** (chosen at level 1 — represents orc focus from 3e)
- **Giants** (chosen at level 6)

Advantage on Wisdom (Survival) checks to track favored enemies and Intelligence checks to recall information about them. Learns one language spoken by favored enemies (Giant).

### Natural Explorer
Hank is particularly familiar with two types of natural environments:
- **Forest** (chosen at level 1)
- **Mountain** (chosen at level 6)

When in a favored terrain: can't become lost by nonmagical means, alert to danger even while foraging or tracking, can move stealthily at normal pace when alone, find twice as much food, learn exact number and size of creatures when tracking.

### Fighting Style: Archery
+2 bonus to attack rolls with ranged weapons (included in attack bonuses above).

### Primeval Awareness
Expend a spell slot to sense whether any aberrations, celestials, dragons, elementals, fey, fiends, or undead are present within 1 mile (6 miles in favored terrain) for 1 minute per slot level. This reveals presence only, not location.

### Extra Attack
Attacks twice when taking the Attack action.

### Hunter Archetype

**Hunter's Prey — Colossus Slayer (level 3):** Once per turn, when you hit a creature with a weapon attack, if the creature is below its hit point maximum, deal an extra 1d8 damage of the weapon's type.

**Defensive Tactics — Escape the Horde (level 7):** Opportunity attacks against you are made with disadvantage.

- Attacking at long range doesn't impose disadvantage.
- Ranged weapon attacks ignore half and three-quarters cover.
- Before making a ranged attack, can choose to take –5 to the attack roll to add +10 to the damage roll.

*Captures Hank's 3e Power Shot mechanic, Point Blank Shot, Precise Shot, and Weapon Focus (longbow).*

## Energy Bow

*Weapon (longbow), very rare (requires attunement)*

Simply drawing your fingers in the air near this finely crafted bow causes it to be strung with an arrow of glimmering energy.

The energy bow functions as a **+2 longbow** that requires no ammunition. It fires arrows of pure magical force that deal **2d6 force damage** instead of the normal 1d8 piercing. Because the arrows are force effects, they have no miss chance against incorporeal creatures and ignore resistance to piercing damage. When drawn, the bow sheds **bright light in a 20-foot radius** and dim light for an additional 20 feet.

**Power Shot.** Before making an attack roll with the energy bow, you may choose to subtract any number up to your proficiency bonus from the attack roll. If the attack hits, add that same number to the damage roll. This decision is made before the roll.

*The Sharpshooter feat's –5/+10 option and the Power Shot feature can both be declared, but the Power Shot bonus is capped at your proficiency bonus (+3).*

The bow can also fire normal or magical arrows, but in those cases it deals the arrow's normal damage rather than force damage, and the +2 bonus still applies.
