# Notes

**Portent dice:** Divination's defining feature. At the start of each long rest, bank two d20 rolls. Either result can be substituted for any attack roll, ability check, or saving throw made by Presto or any creature he can see — including enemies. Save a 2 and a 3, then guarantee a Hypnotic Pattern or Hold Person sticks against a boss. This effectively bypasses the game's most punishing saving throw checks on tactician.

**Spell priority:** Presto is a control caster first and a damage dealer when nothing needs locking down. Cast order on a typical hard fight: Shield of Faith or Bless via a party member first, then Hypnotic Pattern (using a Portent die to guarantee the save fails if needed), then Slow or Hold Person to pin anything that resisted. Fireball and Chain Lightning are for cleanup when enemies are already incapacitated.

**Alert feat:** Initiative determines whether Presto gets Hypnotic Pattern down before enemies act. On tactician, a slow Presto who acts after three enemies is a dead Presto. Alert at level 4 solves this. His Portent dice also reset on a long rest, so acting first every fight is mandatory to get full value from them.

**Concentration management:** Hypnotic Pattern, Slow, Hold Monster, and Haste are all concentration. Presto can only maintain one. Cloak of the Weave and a high CON score make it harder to break. If running Haste on an ally, do not also cast a control spell — losing Haste concentration inflicts Lethargy.

**Weave item set:** Cloak of the Weave and Hood of the Weave together add +2 to spell attack rolls and +2 to Spell Save DC. At level 12 with INT 20, this pushes Spell Save DC to 19 and makes most saving throws academic even without Portent assistance.

**Markoheshkir:** The Act 3 staff grants free casts of its element per short rest and adds +1 to Spell Save DC. Pick the Arcane charge (free Arcane spell) for maximum flexibility, or Cold for synergy with Ray of Frost and Ice Storm.

**The hat problem:** Presto's hat is essential to the character. Birthright is the mechanical best-in-slot helmet (+2 INT = +1 to attack rolls, save DC, and spell damage). The Hood of the Weave is the better thematic stand-in — a wizard's hood feels right. Choose based on whether you need the Weave set bonus or the raw INT.
